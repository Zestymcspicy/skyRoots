The Wheel

There are many nations and various colors (and even direction) are used to signify the Thunders or Winds or Guiding Spirits, but agreement exists that they have power, influence, significance. For Potawatomi, things always begin in the East, designated by the color White, and travel around the circle clockwise. East is Spring, birth, babyhood, beginnings. Look to the east when you begin your morning. Enter the arena at the east. In this game with white markers, like little children, there are more, but they can do less. South, shown with Red, is summer, "where the heat comes from," time of intense growth. Adolescence. West is Yellow for harvest and the lightning that accompanies autumn storms. Adulthood, maturity. A yellow line signifying West divides north from south in many depictions of the wheel. North is symbolized with black, sometimes blue. "Old Man Winter," wisdom, old age, and inevitably, death, but for the Potawatomis, that is not the end because like winter is always followed by spring, East newness always follows North's loss. This phenomenon is signified in the game by the ability to Restore markers that were lost. Winter was also traditionally the time for storytelling and playing games since getting out to work was difficult due to weather. In the game of Thunders, this theme of contrast and complement is conveyed on many levels. Look for things that have their opposites, and the balance that occurs when they begin to interact.

Welcome to the Four Thunders board game!

Four Thunder's board is made up of modular tiles that form together into "the Land" as the game is being played, then in the second half of the game, are taken apart one tile at a time until the Land is no more. This feature of waxing and waning (like the moon) is the first evidence of the life cycle theme of Thunders, drawn from the Nyew Jigwe'k or the four Directions, as known by my tribe, the Prairie Band Potawatomi. More information about the cultural aspects and inspiration for the Thunders game can be found in the Culture section elsewhere on this site in the near future.
